Artificial intelligence has emerged as a transformative force in modern healthcare, revolutionizing disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient care management. Over the past decade, machine learning algorithms have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in analyzing medical imaging, predicting patient outcomes, and identifying patterns that might escape human observation.

AI's most significant applications in healthcare include medical imaging analysis, drug discovery, and predictive analytics. Deep learning models can now detect cancerous tumors, identify fractures, and diagnose conditions with accuracy rates matching experienced radiologists. AI algorithms analyze vast databases of molecular structures to identify promising drug candidates faster than traditional methods. Predictive analytics enable healthcare providers to identify high-risk patients for conditions like heart disease and diabetes, allowing for earlier preventive interventions.

However, AI integration in healthcare faces notable challenges including privacy concerns, algorithmic bias, and regulatory uncertainty. Questions remain about the "black box" nature of AI decision-making and liability when errors occur. Despite these challenges, researchers are developing explainable AI and federated learning approaches to address these issues, promising a future where AI augments human medical expertise to improve outcomes and accessibility.
