Artificial intelligence has emerged as a transformative force in modern healthcare, revolutionizing how medical professionals diagnose diseases, develop treatment plans, and manage patient care. Over the past decade, machine learning algorithms have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in analyzing medical imaging, predicting patient outcomes, and identifying patterns that escape human observation.

AI is making significant impacts across several key applications in healthcare. In medical imaging analysis, deep learning models can detect cancerous tumors, identify fractures, and diagnose conditions like diabetic retinopathy with accuracy rates matching or exceeding experienced radiologists. AI is also accelerating drug discovery by analyzing molecular structures and predicting compound interactions, playing a crucial role during the COVID-19 pandemic. Additionally, predictive analytics enables earlier intervention for high-risk patients by analyzing electronic health records, genetic information, and lifestyle factors.

However, integrating AI in healthcare presents challenges. Privacy concerns arise as these systems require sensitive patient data, and algorithmic bias may cause poor performance for underrepresented populations. The "black box" nature of some AI algorithms makes it difficult for doctors to understand decision-making processes. Regulatory frameworks are still evolving, and questions remain about liability when AI systems make errors. Despite these challenges, researchers are developing explainable AI and federated learning approaches to address these issues, promising a future where AI augments human medical expertise to improve outcomes and accessibility.
