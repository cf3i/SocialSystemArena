Artificial intelligence has emerged as a transformative force in healthcare, revolutionizing disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient care over the past decade. Machine learning algorithms have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in analyzing medical imaging, predicting patient outcomes, and identifying patterns that might escape human observation.

AI applications in healthcare include medical imaging analysis where deep learning models detect cancerous tumors, fractures, and conditions like diabetic retinopathy with 94% accuracy matching experienced radiologists. AI is also accelerating drug discovery by analyzing molecular structures and predicting compound interactions, playing a crucial role during COVID-19. Predictive analytics enables earlier intervention for high-risk patients by analyzing electronic health records, genetic information, and lifestyle factors.

Challenges remain including privacy concerns, algorithmic bias from non-diverse datasets, and the "black box" nature affecting medical transparency. Regulatory frameworks are evolving while liability questions persist. Despite these challenges, explainable AI and federated learning approaches are emerging to address privacy and transparency issues, promising improved outcomes and more accessible healthcare.
