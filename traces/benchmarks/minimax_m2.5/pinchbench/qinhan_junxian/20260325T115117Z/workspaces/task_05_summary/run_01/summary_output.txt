Artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a transformative force in modern healthcare, revolutionizing disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient care management. Machine learning algorithms have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in analyzing medical imaging, predicting patient outcomes, and identifying patterns that might escape human observation. Over the past decade, AI has transitioned from experimental technology to an integral part of healthcare delivery systems worldwide.

The most significant applications of AI in healthcare include medical imaging analysis, drug discovery, and predictive analytics. In medical imaging, deep learning models can detect cancerous tumors, identify fractures, and diagnose conditions with accuracy matching or exceeding experienced radiologists. AI is also accelerating drug discovery by analyzing molecular databases and predicting compound interactions, significantly reducing development timelines. Furthermore, predictive analytics enables earlier intervention by identifying high-risk patients for conditions like heart disease and diabetes before symptoms appear.

However, AI integration faces notable challenges including privacy concerns regarding patient data, algorithmic bias from non-diverse training datasets, and the "black box" nature of some AI systems that limits doctor understanding of decision-making processes. Regulatory frameworks continue to evolve to address AI medical device approval and liability questions. Despite these challenges, the future looks promising as researchers develop explainable AI and federated learning approaches that maintain patient privacy while improving healthcare outcomes.
