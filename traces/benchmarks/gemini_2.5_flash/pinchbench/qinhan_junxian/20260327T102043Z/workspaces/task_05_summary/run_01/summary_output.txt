Artificial intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing modern healthcare by enhancing disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient management. Machine learning algorithms analyze medical imaging, predict patient outcomes, and identify subtle patterns, significantly improving accuracy and efficiency in various medical fields.

Key applications include advanced medical imaging analysis, where deep learning models detect conditions like cancerous tumors and diabetic retinopathy with high accuracy. AI also accelerates drug discovery by analyzing molecular structures and predicting compound interactions, drastically reducing development time and cost. Furthermore, predictive analytics leverage electronic health records and genetic data to identify high-risk patients, enabling earlier intervention and reducing healthcare costs.

However, AI integration faces challenges such as paramount privacy concerns regarding sensitive patient data, potential algorithmic bias from non-diverse datasets, and the "black box" nature of some AI systems hindering transparency in medical decision-making. Regulatory frameworks are still evolving, and liability questions persist. Despite these hurdles, the future is promising with explainable AI and federated learning, aiming to augment human medical expertise for improved, cost-effective, and accessible global healthcare.
