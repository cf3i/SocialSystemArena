Artificial intelligence (AI) is transforming modern healthcare by revolutionizing disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient management. Machine learning algorithms excel at analyzing medical imaging, predicting patient outcomes, and uncovering subtle patterns often missed by human observation, demonstrating remarkable capabilities over the past decade.

Key applications of AI include advanced medical imaging analysis, where deep learning models accurately detect conditions like cancerous tumors and diabetic retinopathy, often matching or exceeding expert radiologists. AI also significantly accelerates drug discovery by analyzing molecular structures and predicting compound interactions, as seen during the COVID-19 pandemic. Furthermore, predictive analytics leverages electronic health records and genetic data to identify high-risk patients, enabling early intervention and reducing healthcare costs.

However, AI integration faces challenges such as paramount privacy concerns regarding sensitive patient data, potential algorithmic bias from non-diverse datasets, and the "black box" nature of some algorithms hindering medical understanding. Regulatory frameworks are still evolving to address safety, efficacy, and liability. Despite these hurdles, the future is promising with explainable AI and federated learning, emphasizing AI's role as a tool to augment human medical expertise for improved global healthcare outcomes.
