Artificial intelligence has emerged as a transformative force in modern healthcare, revolutionizing medical diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient care management. Over the past decade, machine learning algorithms have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in analyzing complex medical data, recognizing patterns that may escape human observation, and enhancing clinical decision-making processes.

The key applications of AI in healthcare span three major domains. In medical imaging, deep learning models can detect tumors, fractures, and diseases like diabetic retinopathy with accuracy matching or exceeding experienced radiologists. In drug discovery, AI accelerates the traditionally lengthy and expensive development process by analyzing molecular structures and predicting compound interactions, as demonstrated during the COVID-19 pandemic. Additionally, predictive analytics enables early intervention by identifying high-risk patients for conditions like heart disease and hospital readmission through analysis of electronic health records and genetic data.

Despite its promise, AI integration faces significant challenges including patient privacy concerns, algorithmic bias from non-diverse training datasets, and the "black box" problem where AI reasoning lacks transparency. Regulatory frameworks are still evolving, with agencies like the FDA developing new approval guidelines for AI-based medical devices. Looking forward, researchers are advancing explainable AI and federated learning to address these issues, while emphasizing that successful healthcare delivery will combine AI's data processing capabilities with human providers' empathy and ethical judgment.
